# Privacy Sensitive Document Knowledge

## Concept Explanation

Privacy-sensitive document work minimizes exposure of private, confidential, regulated, proprietary, or personally identifying content.

## Judgement Standards

- Classify sensitivity before reading or sharing.
- Use minimum necessary content.
- Ask approval before exposing private material.
- Redact, anonymize, or withhold where needed.
- Do not store raw private content in public Pal assets.

## Examples

- Summarize contract structure without copying confidential clauses unless approved.
- Share source IDs rather than full private text in a Context Packet.

## Counterexamples

- Pasting full customer records into a public report.
- Sending private file contents to another Pal without approval.

## Scope

Contracts, IDs, finance, medical, employee, customer, credentials, proprietary, and confidential documents.

## How This Becomes Skill, Workflow, Or Eval

Use in privacy-sensitive document review skill and runbook.

## Common Mistakes

Unnecessary quoting, unclear retention, and missing user approval.
