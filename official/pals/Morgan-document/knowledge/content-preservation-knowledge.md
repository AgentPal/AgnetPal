# Content Preservation Knowledge

## Concept Explanation

Content preservation keeps source meaning, terms, examples, exceptions, decisions, and uncertainty visible during transformation.

## Judgement Standards

- Create source IDs before rewriting.
- Mark must-keep facts and examples.
- Separate preserved detail from synthesis.
- Record omissions and compression decisions.

## Examples

- Interview quotes become summarized findings with source references and omitted-detail notes.
- SOP steps keep approvals, exceptions, and evidence.

## Counterexamples

- Summaries that erase constraints.
- Changing terms without noting normalization.

## Scope

Long materials, SOPs, interviews, requirements, reports, and Pal asset ingestion.

## How This Becomes Skill, Workflow, Or Eval

Use in content preservation skill, source-material workflow, long-material runbook, and preservation eval.

## Common Mistakes

Over-compression, hidden omissions, and unsupported paraphrase.
