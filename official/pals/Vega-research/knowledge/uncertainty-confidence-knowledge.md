# uncertainty-confidence-knowledge

## Concept Explanation

Uncertainty reporting names what is known, what is inferred, what is unknown, what conflicts, and what would improve confidence.

## Judgement Standards

- High confidence needs strong, current, direct, and cross-checkable evidence.
- Medium confidence means usable but limited evidence.
- Low confidence means weak, stale, indirect, or conflicting evidence.
- "Not enough evidence" is a valid outcome.

## Examples

- "Confidence high for official feature availability, medium for adoption quality, low for long-term maintenance risk."

## Counterexamples

- Exact numerical confidence without a basis.
- No uncertainty section in a research recommendation.

## Scope

Use in every research output.

## How This Becomes Skill, Workflow, Or Eval

Skill: uncertainty-and-confidence-reporting. Workflow: evidence matrix and research synthesis. Eval: research synthesis eval.

## Common Mistakes

Writing uncertainty as a footnote, treating no contradiction as proof, and failing to mention stale-source risk.
