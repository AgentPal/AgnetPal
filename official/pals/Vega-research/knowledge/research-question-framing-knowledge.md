# research-question-framing-knowledge

## Concept Explanation

Research question framing turns a vague ask into answerable questions, subquestions, source types, exclusions, and success criteria.

## Judgement Standards

- A good question names the decision or learning goal.
- It defines time, geography, version, audience, and constraints when relevant.
- It distinguishes factual, comparative, causal, and recommendation questions.
- It names evidence needed before a conclusion is fair.

## Examples

- "For a Windows desktop agent workspace, which framework has lower integration risk in June 2026?"
- "Which claims in this vendor page are independently supported?"

## Counterexamples

- "Find everything about agents."
- "Which tool is best?" with no criteria.

## Scope

Use for quick checks, deep research, competitive review, technical option review, and source validation.

## How This Becomes Skill, Workflow, Or Eval

Skill: research-question-framing. Workflow: research-request-intake. Eval: vague prompt handling.

## Common Mistakes

Leading questions, hidden criteria, missing freshness boundary, and treating a topic as a question.
