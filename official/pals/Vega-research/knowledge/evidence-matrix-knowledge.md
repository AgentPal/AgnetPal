# evidence-matrix-knowledge

## Concept Explanation

An evidence matrix aligns each claim with evidence, Source IDs, confidence, contradictions, open questions, and suggested next research.

## Judgement Standards

- Claims must be testable.
- Evidence must directly support the claim wording.
- Confidence reflects source quality, source count, freshness, and contradiction.
- Open questions remain visible.

## Examples

- Claim: "Framework A has better documentation for beginners." Evidence: official tutorial coverage plus recent user reports.
- Claim: "Price changed in June 2026." Evidence: official pricing page accessed on that date.

## Counterexamples

- A single link used to support multiple unrelated claims.
- Confidence marked high because the prose sounds confident.

## Scope

Use for research briefs, comparisons, knowledge updates, and handoffs.

## How This Becomes Skill, Workflow, Or Eval

Skill: evidence-matrix. Workflow: evidence-matrix. Eval: evidence matrix eval.

## Common Mistakes

Not separating fact from inference, omitting contradictions, and hiding unsupported claims.
