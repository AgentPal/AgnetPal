# knowledge-distillation

## Purpose

Convert research findings into durable Pal knowledge, skill, workflow, eval, or decision candidates without copying source text or private material.

## When to use

Use after research has produced reusable lessons that may improve a Pal, workflow, product decision, or documentation.

## Inputs

Research brief, source inventory, evidence matrix, intended owner Pal, privacy boundary, copyright boundary, and update target.

## Required context

Knowledge distillation knowledge, PalSmith governance method, content preservation protocol, and writeback boundary.

## Process

1. Decide whether the finding is reusable or only situational.
2. Map the finding to knowledge, skill, workflow, eval, runbook, or decision note.
3. Preserve source metadata and summarize rather than copy.
4. Identify owner Pal and review needed.
5. Mark private or non-public material as excluded.
6. Produce a writeback candidate for PalSmith or the owning Pal.

## Output

Knowledge update candidate with source IDs, target owner, target file, distilled lesson, exclusions, and review needs.

## Quality bar

The candidate must be useful without requiring the reader to trust unsupported memory.

## User confirmation points

Ask before writing durable knowledge from private, proprietary, sensitive, or controversial material.

## No-code boundary

This skill creates Markdown/JSON candidates only. It does not create tools, indexes by automation, or runtime memory systems.

## Common mistakes

- Copying whole source passages.
- Writing temporary facts as permanent knowledge.
- Assigning the wrong owner Pal.
- Omitting source date and confidence.

## Example

"Distill current AI research citation practice into Vega source inventory rules; PalSmith reviews before release."
