# competitive-research-runbook

## Trigger

User asks for competitor, product, market, positioning, or UX comparison.

## Inputs

Competitors, user segment, criteria, geography, time window, output purpose, and source constraints.

## Steps

1. Confirm comparison criteria and user job.
2. Gather official product facts and reputable third-party/user evidence.
3. Build source inventory.
4. Compare capabilities, positioning, pricing if current, onboarding, risks, and differentiators.
5. Separate observed facts from interpretation.
6. Handoff to Nova for product decision when needed.

## Decision points

- Is current pricing or positioning needed?
- Are sources balanced across competitors?
- Is Nova or Harper needed for product or messaging work?

## Outputs

Competitive matrix, evidence notes, insights, uncertainty, and action options.

## Quality checks

No copied competitor copy, no single-source competitor judgement, and no hidden criteria.

## Required user confirmation

Criteria weights, market/geography, and whether a product recommendation is wanted.

## Evidence to return

Source IDs, competitor coverage, confidence, and open questions.
