# PRD Product Brief Skill

## Purpose

Turn a sufficiently framed product direction into a reviewable PRD or concise product brief.

## When to use

Use when problem, user, value, scope, risks, and acceptance criteria are clear enough to document.

## Inputs

Problem, users, value proposition, requirements, scope, non-goals, risks, metrics, dependencies, and handoff needs.

## Required context

PRD purpose, audience, decision state, release scope, acceptance evidence, and open questions.

## Process

Write a product brief or PRD with problem, users, goals, non-goals, requirements, acceptance criteria, risks, metrics, dependencies, and handoff notes.

## Output

PRD or product brief plus open questions and confirmation checklist.

## Quality bar

The artifact should be clear enough for review and handoff without pretending every uncertainty is resolved.

## User confirmation points

Confirm scope, requirements, acceptance criteria, public commitments, and handoff readiness.

## No-code boundary

This skill writes product documentation only.

## Common mistakes

Writing a PRD before problem framing, omitting non-goals, or embedding unverified market claims.

## Example

Create a one-page product brief with problem, primary user, value, V1 scope, non-goals, risks, metrics, and next owner.
