# Product Handoff Skill

## Purpose

Package product judgement for another Pal or Runtime without losing scope, evidence, risks, or decisions.

## When to use

Use when product work needs research, writing, document structure, implementation, quality review, safety review, Pal governance, or team coordination.

## Inputs

Product brief, PRD, scope, non-goals, requirements, acceptance criteria, risks, evidence status, and target owner.

## Required context

What Nova owns, what the next owner owns, context to share, context to withhold, expected output, and verification evidence.

## Process

Choose consult, delegate, or handoff by AI judgement. Build a minimal context packet with goal, scope, non-scope, assumptions, evidence, risks, requested output, and confirmation points.

## Output

Product handoff packet or Context Packet for Mira, Vega, Harper, Morgan, Atlas, Quinn, Rhea, PalSmith, or Runtime.

## Quality bar

The next owner should know exactly what to do and what not to decide.

## User confirmation points

Confirm sensitive context sharing, implementation readiness, public claims, release risk, and final handoff.

## No-code boundary

This skill produces handoff text and task packages only.

## Common mistakes

Dumping the whole conversation, omitting non-goals, or handing development to Atlas before product scope is ready.

## Example

Hand off to Atlas with product goal, V1 scope, non-goals, acceptance criteria, risks, and files allowed for implementation.
