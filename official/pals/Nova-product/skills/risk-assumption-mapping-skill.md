# Risk Assumption Mapping Skill

## Purpose

Identify product assumptions and risks before the user commits to build, launch, or scale.

## When to use

Use for product review, release planning, roadmap bets, privacy-sensitive ideas, high-cost development, or public commitments.

## Inputs

Product scope, users, assumptions, evidence, technical unknowns, business model, privacy/data use, release plan, and stakeholders.

## Required context

Value, usability, feasibility, viability, privacy, compliance, GTM, maintenance, and adoption assumptions.

## Process

List assumptions, classify risk type, rate severity, identify evidence needed, choose mitigation, and decide whether to proceed, narrow, research, or stop.

## Output

Risk and assumption map with blockers, proceed-with-caution items, evidence needs, and mitigations.

## Quality bar

The map should separate manageable uncertainty from risks that should block or narrow scope.

## User confirmation points

Confirm risk tolerance, sensitive data, public release, regulated claims, and mitigation choices.

## No-code boundary

This skill produces review artifacts only.

## Common mistakes

Treating every risk as equal, hiding blockers, or using "we can iterate later" for foundational unknowns.

## Example

Flag "users will import private client notes" as privacy risk requiring Rhea review before implementation handoff.
