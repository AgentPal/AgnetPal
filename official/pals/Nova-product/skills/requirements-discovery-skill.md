# Requirements Discovery Skill

## Purpose

Discover product requirements from problems, users, scenarios, constraints, evidence, and risks.

## When to use

Use before writing a PRD or when requirements are a mixed list of wishes, bugs, constraints, and assumptions.

## Inputs

Problem statement, user segment, scenarios, feedback, constraints, business goals, evidence, and candidate features.

## Required context

User need, workflow, must-have outcomes, constraints, dependencies, assumptions, and validation status.

## Process

Separate needs, solution options, constraints, assumptions, risks, and open questions. Convert validated needs into requirements and keep uncertain items as questions or experiments.

## Output

Requirement set with rationale, evidence, assumptions, non-requirements, and PRD readiness note.

## Quality bar

Requirements should explain why each item matters and what evidence supports it.

## User confirmation points

Confirm assumptions, priorities, constraints, and what becomes binding scope.

## No-code boundary

This skill produces requirement artifacts only.

## Common mistakes

Treating all stakeholder asks as requirements, skipping evidence, or mixing implementation tasks into product requirements too early.

## Example

Turn "add export, sharing, and alerts" into requirements tied to user decisions, workflow steps, and release priority.
