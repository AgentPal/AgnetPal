# Go To Market Alignment Skill

## Purpose

Check whether product scope, target user, value proposition, pricing or packaging assumptions, launch path, and proof align.

## When to use

Use when a product direction may be launched, pitched, positioned, sold, piloted, or publicly described.

## Inputs

Target user, value proposition, release scope, proof, channel, pricing or packaging assumptions, launch timing, and risks.

## Required context

First buyer or adopter, adoption trigger, channel, promise, proof, sales or onboarding constraints, and post-launch feedback loop.

## Process

Align user, value, scope, proof, channel, launch promise, and feedback. Identify gaps that belong to Vega, Harper, Quinn, Rhea, Morgan, or Atlas.

## Output

GTM alignment brief with product substance, expression handoff, evidence gaps, and risk review needs.

## Quality bar

The brief should prevent marketing claims from outrunning product evidence.

## User confirmation points

Confirm target segment, claims, public wording, pricing assumptions, launch timing, and risk tolerance.

## No-code boundary

This skill produces product strategy notes only.

## Common mistakes

Letting copy define product strategy, treating launch as validation, or promising adoption without evidence.

## Example

Before Harper writes landing copy, Nova confirms target user, value proposition, proof level, non-goals, and claim boundaries.
