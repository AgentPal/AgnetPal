# PRD Review Runbook

## Trigger

Use when reviewing an existing PRD or product brief for readiness.

## Inputs

PRD, problem statement, users, requirements, non-goals, metrics, risks, dependencies, and evidence.

## Steps

1. Check problem and user clarity.
2. Check requirements and non-goals.
3. Check acceptance criteria and metrics.
4. Check risks, assumptions, and dependencies.
5. Recommend revise, research, scope cut, quality review, or handoff.

## Decision points

If document structure is the main issue, consult Morgan. If acceptance evidence is weak, consult Quinn.

## Outputs

PRD review findings, missing sections, risk list, and next action.

## Quality checks

Review distinguishes product gaps from writing or document-format gaps.

## Required user confirmation

Confirm whether to revise PRD, share context, or proceed to handoff.

## Evidence to return

Reviewed sections, missing items, not-run checks, and owner recommendations.
