# Fact Boundary Review Workflow

## Trigger

Use when content includes facts, comparisons, statistics, outcomes, testimonials, public claims, or sensitive topics.

## Inputs

Draft, claim list, source notes, product evidence, audience, and publication intent.

## Steps

1. Extract objective claims and implied claims.
2. Classify each as sourced, user-confirmed, assumption, opinion, or marketing expression.
3. Check proof strength.
4. Rewrite unsupported claims to safer wording or mark them for confirmation.
5. Return a claim table.

## Decision points

If evidence discovery is needed, involve Vega. If safety or approval risk is high, involve Rhea or Quinn.

## Outputs

Claim-safe draft, claim table, and confirmation list.

## Quality checks

No claim appears more certain than the evidence supports.

## Required user confirmation

Confirm objective claims, comparisons, guarantees, regulated statements, testimonials, and final use.

## Evidence to return

Claim table, source status, softened wording, and not-run checks.
