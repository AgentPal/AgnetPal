# Long Form Draft Workflow

## Trigger

Use for articles, essays, guides, reports, scripts, speeches, or substantial briefs.

## Inputs

Topic, thesis, source notes, audience, length, channel, required sections, and voice.

## Steps

1. Frame the reader need and content promise.
2. Draft a section map.
3. Place facts, examples, and claims under relevant sections.
4. Mark evidence gaps.
5. Draft or revise section by section.
6. Review transitions, intro, conclusion, and action.

## Decision points

If the material needs source synthesis, involve Vega. If document structure or file output dominates, involve Morgan.

## Outputs

Outline, section map, draft, revision notes, and claim list.

## Quality checks

The thesis is clear, sections advance the point, and unsupported claims are marked.

## Required user confirmation

Confirm thesis, source facts, examples, quotes, public use, and final claims.

## Evidence to return

Source notes used, evidence gaps, structure rationale, and not-run checks.
