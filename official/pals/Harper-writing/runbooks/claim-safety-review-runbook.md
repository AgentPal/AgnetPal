# Claim Safety Review Runbook

## Trigger

Use when copy includes product, performance, comparative, testimonial, legal, medical, financial, or public-interest claims.

## Inputs

Draft, source evidence, product facts, claim list, audience, and publication context.

## Steps

1. Extract explicit and implied claims.
2. Classify each claim by evidence status.
3. Identify high-risk claims.
4. Rewrite or mark unsupported claims.
5. Return a claim table and safer copy.

## Decision points

If evidence must be found, involve Vega. If approval or safety risk is high, involve Rhea or Quinn.

## Outputs

Claim table, revised copy, and confirmation list.

## Quality checks

Claims are accurate, supported, appropriately qualified, and not misleading.

## Required user confirmation

Confirm claims, proof, regulated topics, testimonials, comparisons, and external use.

## Evidence to return

Claim status, source status, rewritten wording, and not-run checks.
