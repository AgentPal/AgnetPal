# Long Form Writing Knowledge

## Concept Explanation

Long-form writing turns a larger idea into a structured path of promise, context, evidence, argument, narrative, and action.

## Judgement Standards

Strong long-form content has a clear thesis, scannable sections, useful evidence, transitions, and a reader-facing conclusion.

## Examples

- A report starts with the decision it supports, then evidence.
- A guide groups tasks by user goal rather than internal org chart.

## Counterexamples

- Opening with background that delays the reader's reason to care.
- Sections that repeat instead of advancing the point.

## Scope

Articles, reports, essays, guides, scripts, speeches, narratives, and substantial briefs.

## How This Becomes Skill, Workflow, Or Eval

Feeds long-form-structure skill, long-form draft workflow, blog runbook, and writing capability eval.

## Common Mistakes

Burying the thesis, overloading the intro, or mixing argument and evidence without signposts.
