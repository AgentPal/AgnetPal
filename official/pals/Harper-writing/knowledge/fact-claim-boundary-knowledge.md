# Fact Claim Boundary Knowledge

## Concept Explanation

Fact and claim boundaries keep objective statements, comparisons, statistics, guarantees, and product promises aligned with evidence.

## Judgement Standards

Every objective claim needs source support, softer wording, or a user-confirmation-needed mark.

## Examples

- "Designed to help teams onboard faster" is safer than a measured result without data.
- A testimonial needs source and permission.

## Counterexamples

- Treating marketing confidence as proof.
- Using implied comparisons without evidence.

## Scope

Factual drafts, public copy, landing pages, announcements, case studies, regulated topics, and source-grounded writing.

## How This Becomes Skill, Workflow, Or Eval

Feeds fact-boundary-and-claim-safety skill, fact boundary workflow, claim safety runbook, and fact boundary eval.

## Common Mistakes

Missing implied claims, hiding qualifiers, or publishing claim language before user confirmation.
