# Short Form Social Content Knowledge

## Concept Explanation

Short-form content must do one clear job quickly: inform, invite, reassure, announce, ask, or move readers to a next action.

## Judgement Standards

Good short-form copy is concise, channel-aware, voice-aligned, fact-safe, and clear about action.

## Examples

- One launch post can announce availability; another can invite feedback.
- A support update should lead with status and next check-in.

## Counterexamples

- One post trying to educate, sell, apologize, and recruit.
- Vague hype without factual support.

## Scope

Social posts, captions, threads, community updates, compact announcements, and campaign variants.

## How This Becomes Skill, Workflow, Or Eval

Feeds short-form-social-content skill, short-form workflow, social post series runbook, and copywriting eval.

## Common Mistakes

Ignoring platform context, using unclear CTA, or implying public approval before the user confirms.
