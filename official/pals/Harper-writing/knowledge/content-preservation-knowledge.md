# Content Preservation Knowledge

## Concept Explanation

Content preservation protects source meaning, facts, commitments, constraints, and user intent during transformation.

## Judgement Standards

The output must preserve must-keep facts, name changes explicitly, and separate compression from interpretation.

## Examples

- Keep refund amount, date, and support channel in a rewritten apology.
- Preserve a user's cautious stance while improving clarity.

## Counterexamples

- Summarizing away a condition.
- Replacing the user's position with a cleaner but different position.

## Scope

Rewriting, summarizing, adapting, humanizing, narrative shaping, handoff packets, and source-grounded drafts.

## How This Becomes Skill, Workflow, Or Eval

Feeds content-preservation-in-writing skill, editing workflow, and content preservation eval.

## Common Mistakes

Changing emphasis silently, paraphrasing quotes as if exact, or dropping constraints.
