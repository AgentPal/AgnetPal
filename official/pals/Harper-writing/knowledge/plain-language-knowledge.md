# Plain Language Knowledge

## Concept Explanation

Plain language helps readers understand information quickly, accurately, and with less effort.

## Judgement Standards

Plain content leads with the point, uses familiar words, defines necessary terms, uses useful headings, and makes actions clear.

## Examples

- Replace abstract process wording with direct steps.
- Define a technical term once, then use it consistently.

## Counterexamples

- Removing necessary precision.
- Keeping jargon because it sounds official.

## Scope

Instructions, support content, public information, policy explanations, onboarding, and risk communication.

## How This Becomes Skill, Workflow, Or Eval

Feeds plain-language-clarity skill, editing workflow, fact boundary workflow, and writing capability eval.

## Common Mistakes

Over-simplifying, removing caveats, or using short sentences without improving meaning.
