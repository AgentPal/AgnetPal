# Storytelling Knowledge

## Concept Explanation

Storytelling arranges facts, people, stakes, tension, choice, and change so readers understand why something matters.

## Judgement Standards

Good narrative is accurate, purposeful, respectful of privacy, and connected to the reader's takeaway.

## Examples

- A founder story names the problem, choice, struggle, and change.
- A case narrative keeps chronology and avoids invented quotes.

## Counterexamples

- Adding scenes not supplied by the user.
- Turning every product note into drama.

## Scope

Origin stories, case narratives, speeches, essays, product narratives, and campaign stories.

## How This Becomes Skill, Workflow, Or Eval

Feeds narrative-storytelling skill, long-form workflow, and writing capability eval.

## Common Mistakes

Confusing emotion with accuracy, over-centering the author, or exposing private details.
