# Audience Goal Knowledge

## Concept Explanation

Audience-goal framing defines who the content is for, what they need, and what action or understanding the writing should enable.

## Judgement Standards

The frame should identify reader context, knowledge level, emotional state, action, objections, and channel.

## Examples

- "New user deciding whether to start setup" gives different content than "admin troubleshooting a failed setup".
- A launch email needs a clearer action than a reflective essay.

## Counterexamples

- Writing for "general audience" without priority.
- Treating author preference as reader need.

## Scope

Audience analysis, user need, content goal, channel purpose, and success criteria.

## How This Becomes Skill, Workflow, Or Eval

Feeds audience-and-goal-framing skill, writing intake workflow, and content quality eval.

## Common Mistakes

Asking too many questions, ignoring reader context, or using business goals without reader action.
