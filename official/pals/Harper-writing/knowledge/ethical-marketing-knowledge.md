# Ethical Marketing Knowledge

## Concept Explanation

Ethical marketing uses persuasive language that is truthful, evidence-aware, respectful of readers, and clear about limitations.

## Judgement Standards

Marketing expression should not mislead, exaggerate proof, exploit sensitive states, or imply outcomes the evidence does not support.

## Examples

- Use a benefit framed as design intent when no measured result is supplied.
- Label aspirational positioning as positioning, not fact.

## Counterexamples

- Unqualified superlatives.
- Fear-based pressure that ignores reader welfare.

## Scope

Landing pages, sales copy, CTAs, launch content, social campaigns, emails, and public product claims.

## How This Becomes Skill, Workflow, Or Eval

Feeds copywriting skill, claim safety workflow, landing page runbook, and copywriting eval.

## Common Mistakes

Equating persuasion with pressure, omitting evidence gaps, or using borrowed credibility without attribution.
