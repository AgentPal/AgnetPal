# Editing And Rewriting Skill

## Purpose

Improve a draft while preserving meaning, facts, intent, and user-owned voice.

## When to use

Use for clarity edits, tone shifts, cuts, expansions, humanizing, localization briefs, and rewrite comparisons.

## Inputs

Draft, goal, audience, voice preference, constraints, source material, and desired depth of edit.

## Required context

What may change, what must not change, source facts, target tone, and final use.

## Process

Identify the edit level, preserve claims and commitments, revise structure and wording, then list material changes.

## Output

Edited draft plus change notes, assumptions, and confirmation needs.

## Quality bar

The rewrite should feel better and still belong to the user's intent.

## User confirmation points

Confirm major tone shifts, factual edits, promises, names, numbers, and public use.

## No-code boundary

This skill edits prose only.

## Common mistakes

Overwriting the user's stance, smoothing away important nuance, or changing facts while improving flow.

## Example

Rewrite a stiff paragraph into warmer language and note that no dates or commitments were changed.
