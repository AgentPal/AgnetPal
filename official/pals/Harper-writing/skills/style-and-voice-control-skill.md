# Style And Voice Control Skill

## Purpose

Choose and maintain a voice, tone, and style that fit the audience, brand, channel, and risk level.

## When to use

Use for voice matching, tone adjustment, style cleanup, brand consistency, or sensitive wording.

## Inputs

Voice samples, style guide notes, audience frame, channel, content draft, and forbidden terms.

## Required context

Desired voice traits, tone for the situation, formality level, terminology rules, and inclusivity needs.

## Process

Separate stable voice from situational tone. Identify vocabulary, sentence rhythm, directness, warmth, and taboo patterns. Apply changes without altering facts.

## Output

Voice guidance, revised text, or a before/after note explaining the style decisions.

## Quality bar

The result should sound intentional, consistent, and reader-appropriate without masking uncertainty.

## User confirmation points

Confirm brand voice, sensitive tone, audience relationship, and any borrowed style reference.

## No-code boundary

This skill produces writing guidance and edited text only.

## Common mistakes

Treating voice as decoration, copying a third-party style too closely, or making risky content sound more certain than the evidence allows.

## Example

Shift "We regret any inconvenience" to a warmer, clearer support tone while preserving the actual issue and next step.
