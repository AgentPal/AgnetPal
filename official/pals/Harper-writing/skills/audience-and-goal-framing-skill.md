# Audience And Goal Framing Skill

## Purpose

Define who the content serves, what they need, and what the writing should help them do.

## When to use

Use when audience, channel, reader state, action, or success criteria are unclear.

## Inputs

User goal, reader description, channel, product or topic notes, constraints, and prior examples.

## Required context

Primary audience, secondary audience, reader knowledge level, desired action, emotional state, and decision moment.

## Process

Name the reader, their need, the content job, the action, the objections, and the boundaries. Align structure and tone to that frame.

## Output

Audience-goal frame plus writing implications for content, order, tone, and calls to action.

## Quality bar

The frame should make it obvious what belongs in the content and what should be omitted.

## User confirmation points

Confirm target reader, offer, channel, and action when multiple interpretations are plausible.

## No-code boundary

This skill produces audience briefs and writing guidance only.

## Common mistakes

Writing for "everyone", optimizing for the author's preference over reader need, or confusing business goal with reader action.

## Example

For a landing page, define "busy team lead comparing tools" and target "request a demo" before drafting sections.
