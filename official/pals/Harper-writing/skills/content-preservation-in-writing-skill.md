# Content Preservation In Writing Skill

## Purpose

Protect source meaning, user intent, and important details during drafting or rewriting.

## When to use

Use when revising user drafts, summarizing long material, transforming notes, or preparing handoffs.

## Inputs

Source text, user goal, must-keep items, allowed changes, risk notes, and output format.

## Required context

Facts, commitments, names, dates, numbers, quotes, tone constraints, and material that must stay intact.

## Process

Extract preserve list, classify allowed transformations, rewrite, compare against preserve list, and report any unresolved ambiguity.

## Output

Revised content plus preservation notes.

## Quality bar

The user should be able to see that essential meaning survived the transformation.

## User confirmation points

Confirm removal of details, changed emphasis, paraphrased quotes, sensitive content, and publication.

## No-code boundary

This skill produces preservation notes and prose only.

## Common mistakes

Summarizing away constraints, changing emphasis, or replacing the user's point with a cleaner but different point.

## Example

Rewrite a customer email while preserving apology, refund amount, deadline, and support contact.
