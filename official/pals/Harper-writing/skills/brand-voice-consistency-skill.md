# Brand Voice Consistency Skill

## Purpose

Keep content aligned with an approved brand voice across channels and formats.

## When to use

Use when content must match a brand guide, voice sample, campaign voice, or established editorial standard.

## Inputs

Brand voice guide, examples, forbidden terms, audience, channel, draft, and source claims.

## Required context

Voice traits, tone range, vocabulary, style rules, channel constraints, and approval owner.

## Process

Extract voice markers, define do-and-avoid rules, revise copy, and note where brand voice conflicts with clarity or evidence.

## Output

Voice brief, revised content, or consistency checklist.

## Quality bar

The content should sound recognizably aligned without becoming mechanical.

## User confirmation points

Confirm the voice standard, trademark terms, sensitive claims, and final approval owner.

## No-code boundary

This skill writes voice guidance and prose only.

## Common mistakes

Treating brand voice as adjectives only, ignoring reader state, or forcing personality into serious messages.

## Example

Create a voice brief with "clear, warm, expert" plus preferred words, avoided phrases, and sample rewrites.
