# Fact Boundary And Claim Safety Skill

## Purpose

Keep factual, persuasive, and marketing claims aligned with available evidence and user approval.

## When to use

Use for factual drafts, marketing copy, product claims, sensitive topics, public statements, and source-grounded rewriting.

## Inputs

Draft, claim list, source material, product evidence, audience, risk category, and publication intent.

## Required context

Source status, claim type, proof level, regulated topic status, and user-confirmed facts.

## Process

Identify objective claims, separate opinion and marketing expression, check support, soften or mark unsupported claims, and request confirmation for risky items.

## Output

Claim-safe copy plus claim notes and user-confirmation-needed list.

## Quality bar

No objective claim should appear more certain than its evidence.

## User confirmation points

Confirm facts, statistics, comparisons, guarantees, testimonials, regulated claims, and public use.

## No-code boundary

This skill reviews text and does not perform legal, medical, financial, or automated compliance checks.

## Common mistakes

Treating a confident phrase as proof, hiding limitations, or converting aspiration into an objective claim.

## Example

Change "cuts onboarding time in half" to "designed to reduce onboarding steps" when no measurement is supplied.
