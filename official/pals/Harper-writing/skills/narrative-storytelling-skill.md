# Narrative Storytelling Skill

## Purpose

Shape stories that help readers understand change, stakes, people, and meaning.

## When to use

Use for founder stories, case narratives, speeches, essays, launch stories, and campaign narratives.

## Inputs

Events, audience, protagonist, conflict, turning point, evidence, voice, and desired takeaway.

## Required context

What happened, what changed, what is verified, what is private, and what the reader should feel or do.

## Process

Find the narrative arc, protect factual sequence, choose scene or summary, connect emotion to evidence, and avoid invented details.

## Output

Narrative outline, story draft, or rewrite with factual boundary notes.

## Quality bar

The story should be vivid enough to matter and accurate enough to trust.

## User confirmation points

Confirm private details, quotes, identities, chronology, and publication intent.

## No-code boundary

This skill outputs narrative prose and notes only.

## Common mistakes

Adding dramatic details without source support, flattening the human point, or making the story serve the writer more than the reader.

## Example

Turn a product origin note into a concise story with problem, choice, struggle, result, and next promise.
