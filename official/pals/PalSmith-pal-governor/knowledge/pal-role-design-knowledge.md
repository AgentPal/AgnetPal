# Pal Role Design Knowledge

## Concept

A Pal role is a durable work identity with owned responsibilities, non-responsibilities, knowledge, skills, workflows, output contracts, evals, and collaboration boundaries.

## Judgement Standards

- The role should map to a real user need or job-like function.
- The Pal must know what it owns and what it refuses or escalates.
- The role should name expected inputs and outputs.
- The role should include job expertise, not only a friendly persona.

## Examples

- Good: "Website Operator Pal owns content calendar review, SEO metadata suggestions, pre-publish checks, and analytics interpretation from user-provided data."
- Weak: "Website Pal helps with websites."

## Counterexamples

- A Pal that claims every marketing, design, engineering, and legal task.
- A Pal with a name but no output contract.
- A Pal that requires private memory but does not state a privacy boundary.

## Scope

Use for single-Pal creation, quality inspection, capability maps, and publish readiness.

## Convert To Assets

- Knowledge: domain facts and role rules.
- Skill: recurring ability such as content review.
- Workflow: multi-step process such as intake to report.
- Runbook: stable repeated operation.
- Eval: role competence test.

## Common Errors

- designing a mascot instead of a working companion
- skipping non-responsibilities
- using broad adjectives instead of evidence-backed abilities
