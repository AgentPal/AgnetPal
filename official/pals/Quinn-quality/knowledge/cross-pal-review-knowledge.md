# Cross-Pal Review Knowledge

## Concept Explanation

Cross-Pal review checks whether a multi-stage or multi-Pal result preserves ownership, evidence, boundaries, and final quality. Quinn reviews the quality gate, not every specialist job.

## Judgement Standards

- Identify each stage owner.
- Verify evidence for each owner claim.
- Preserve Rhea safety, Vega source uncertainty, PalSmith governance, Atlas implementation evidence, and Mira coordination context.
- Share only necessary context.
- Return missing work to the right owner.

## Examples

- PalSmith designs a Pal upgrade task package; Atlas edits files; Rhea reviews no-code risk; Quinn reviews final evidence.
- Vega provides source inventory; Quinn checks evidence quality and unsupported claims.

## Counterexamples

- Quinn rewrites implementation scope.
- Mira summarizes all work as pass without Quinn evidence review.
- Full private logs are shared across Pals without approval.

## Scope

Use for composite AgentPal work, Pal upgrades, release candidates, research-to-implementation flows, and document/product deliverables requiring independent verification.

## How This Becomes Skill, Workflow, Or Eval

It supports `cross-pal-review-skill.md`, `cross-pal-quality-review-workflow.md`, `collaboration-with-default-pals.md`, and `quinn-cross-pal-review-eval.md`.

## Common Mistakes

- Fixed semantic routing.
- Loss of source IDs or not-run items.
- Quinn becoming universal owner.
