# Workflow Map

## Purpose

This map shows reusable content operations workflow candidates. It is not an
automation graph and does not execute work.

## Workflow Candidates

| Workflow | Candidate owner judgement inputs | Expected evidence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Content request intake | Mira may help clarify goal, audience, format, constraints, source availability, and private-boundary needs. | Intake summary, known sources, unknowns, approval needs. |
| Brief and source pack preparation | Vega may be considered for research planning; Morgan may be considered for document organization. | Source list, uncertainty notes, allowed-use status, not-run items. |
| Draft and edit loop | Harper may be considered for writing craft; Mira may coordinate handoff and progress. | Draft version, edit notes, unresolved choices. |
| Publishing readiness review | Quinn may be considered for verification and release-risk review. | Checklist result, blockers, missing evidence, not-run checks. |
| Learning candidate review | Mira, Quinn, or PalSmith may be considered depending on whether the lesson is coordination, quality, or Org Pack governance. | De-identified learning candidate, destination recommendation, privacy review. |

## Boundaries

- No workflow here is a deterministic route.
- No workflow here calls APIs, publishes content, or modifies customer systems.
- Private customer materials must remain in approved private records.
- Runtime work requires a separate task package and current evidence.
