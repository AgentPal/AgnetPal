# Delivery

## Purpose

This Delivery Pack turns a public-safe business need into a simple project delivery structure:

```text
Project
  -> Flow Pack
  -> Task Package
```

It is written as if Faye, the AI Delivery Pal concept, prepared the package and asked PalSmith for a Pal team skeleton. Faye is a delivery role in this example, not a central roster modification.

## Step 1: Raw user need

A content team wants a short-video growth AI team for product intro, founder talking, customer case, live clip, and publishing review workflows.

## Step 2: Simulated materials / directory notes

The example assumes these public-safe notes:

- product intro materials: missing;
- founder talking points: simulated;
- customer case permission: missing;
- live clip source: simulated timestamp note only;
- publishing profile: notes only, no account access;
- brand voice: missing;
- performance analytics: not-run.

## Step 3: Faye missing information list

- real product description;
- approved brand voice examples;
- customer permission status;
- actual footage inventory;
- platform access policy;
- human approval owner;
- post-publication evidence source.

## Step 4: Faye reasonable assumptions

- use simulated public-safe materials only;
- avoid customer names and private records;
- keep every final content artifact in `requires-human-review` state;
- treat platform and cloud tools as profile notes only;
- ask PalSmith for a skeleton plan, not automatic Pal creation;
- keep candidate Pals as AI judgement inputs, not route rules.

## Step 5: Faye Delivery Pack

Faye produces this package with project facts, five flow files, two task packages, acceptance criteria, training notes, report notes, and machine-readable metadata.

## Step 6: Faye `PAL_TEAM.md`

Faye proposes a candidate team: Research Pal, Scriptwriter Pal, Brand Guardian Pal, Material Manager Pal, Video QA Pal, Publishing Pal, and Growth Review Pal.

## Step 7: Faye `FAYE_BUILD_REQUEST.md`

Faye asks PalSmith to create or propose public-safe Pal team skeletons, required assets, Skill needs, workflow needs, runbooks, memory/report boundaries, and blocked items.

## Step 8: PalSmith creates Pal team skeleton

PalSmith may create a Pal team skeleton only in a separate approved task with current runtime evidence. This example records the request and expected boundary; it does not mutate central contacts or official Pal assets.
