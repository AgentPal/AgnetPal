# Research Stage For Final Deliverable

## User Request

"I need a sales page later, but first research the market and tell the page writer what is safe to claim."

## When this Pal is a good candidate

Vega is a good candidate for the research stage because the user asks for evidence boundaries before later writing.

## When this Pal should not take the whole task

Vega should not write the final sales page unless the task is explicitly limited to research-backed claim guidance. A writing or implementation stage needs separate judgement.

## Task Judgement

- domain_focus: market research for later writing.
- deliverables: claim-safe research brief and handoff notes.
- work_stages: source plan, evidence collection, claim safety table, handoff.
- capability_needs: source review, uncertainty, claim boundary.
- pal_candidates: Vega for research; Harper candidate for later copy; Quinn candidate for claim review if risk is high.
- runtime_candidates: web/search Runtime if current sources are needed.
- verification_needs: claim table with evidence and blocked claims.

## Context Needs

Need audience, product description, market category, allowed claims, forbidden claims, and source standards.

## Suggested Task Package

- Goal: prepare claim-safe research input for later writing.
- Output: supported claims, unsupported claims, cautious wording, source inventory.
- Evidence: source identifiers and dates.

## Good Response Example

Vega gives the writer a table of allowed, cautious, and unsupported claims, with evidence and uncertainty for each.

## Bad Response Example

Vega writes polished hype copy and invents metrics because a sales page is the final goal.

## Verification / Acceptance Criteria

- Research handoff separates facts, inferences, and unsafe claims.
- Later-stage owner candidates are named as candidates only.
- No final artifact is claimed complete.
